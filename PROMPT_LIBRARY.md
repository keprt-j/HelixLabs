# HelixLabs Prompt Library

Use these prompts to demonstrate both domain-specialized and domain-agnostic behavior.

## Chemistry / Materials Prompts

1. Optimize LLZO dopant concentration and temperature to maximize ionic conductivity while preserving phase stability.
2. Tune sintering temperature and dwell time to improve cathode conductivity without increasing crack risk.
3. Identify concentration and annealing conditions that maximize thin-film carrier mobility with acceptable defect density.
4. Optimize electrolyte solvent ratio and salt concentration to improve cycle life while maintaining fast charge performance.
5. Find catalyst loading and reaction temperature settings that maximize conversion and minimize byproduct selectivity.

## General / Software & Operations Prompts

1. Optimize ad bid strategy to maximize weekly conversions under a fixed budget and CPA cap.
2. Tune recommendation ranking to increase click-through while preserving content diversity across user segments.
3. Optimize cloud autoscaling thresholds to reduce cost while keeping p95 latency under 250ms.
4. Find onboarding message timing and sequence that increases 14-day activation without raising unsubscribe rate.
5. Optimize incident triage routing to minimize time-to-resolution while keeping escalation rate below target.

## Reviewer-Focused Prompts

1. Maximize fraud detection recall while keeping false positives below 2%.
2. Optimize support staffing mix per shift to reduce queue time under labor constraints.
3. Tune feature rollout percentage and cooldown intervals to minimize rollback probability.
4. Optimize warehouse pick batching strategy to minimize fulfillment time while preserving accuracy.
5. Improve appointment reminder cadence to reduce no-shows without increasing cancellation rate.

## Stress-Test Prompts (Edge Cases)

1. Improve output quality under strict cost and latency constraints with limited historical data.
2. Optimize two conflicting KPIs when literature evidence is sparse and outcomes are noisy.
3. Identify robust settings when objective performance varies by context and subgroup behavior.
